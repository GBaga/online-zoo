Did you know?

Giant pandas have an insatiable appetite for bamboo. A typical animal eats half the day — a full 12 out of every 24 hours — and relieves itself dozens of times a day. It takes 28 pounds of bamboo to satisfy a giant panda's daily dietary needs.

Giant pandas are very unusual animals that eat almost exclusively bamboo, which is very low in nutrients. Because of this they have a very specialized diet and far too energy-heavy. Giant pandas spend much of their time eating. They have a specially adapted set of characteristics that make bamboo eating possible — their paws are built for gripping bamboo stalks. Estimated fewer than 2,000 giant pandas remain in the wild, though conservation programs have helped their numbers slowly recover.

Bald eagles can fly at speeds of 35-43 mph, and can reach diving speeds up to 100 mph. Their nests, called aeries, can weigh up to 2 tons — the largest nests of any North American bird.

Did you know?

Gorillas are the largest living primates, reaching heights between 1.25 and 1.8 meters. They share 98.3% of their genetic code with humans, making them our closest cousins after chimpanzees and bonobos.

Did you know?

Ring-tailed lemurs spend a lot of time on the ground, which is unusual among lemur species. They can be found in large social groups of up to 30 individuals, called troops, which are led by dominant females.
